A

Grade A — Excellent

Genuine scholarly thinking, well-argued and honestly reflected upon.

Research question
Sharp and specific — clearly answerable within 4,000 words.
Structure
Guides the reader logically from start to finish.
Knowledge
Deep understanding, placed in the wider subject context.
Analysis
Every claim is evidenced. Conclusions follow the argument.
Sources
Well-chosen, correctly cited and critically evaluated.
Reflection (RPPF)
Evaluative — shows clear growth and learning transfer.

Ask yourself

  • Can my RQ be fully answered in exactly 4,000 words?
  • Does every paragraph push my argument forward?
  • Does my RPPF evaluate, not just describe?
B

Grade B — Good

Solid and well-argued, but analysis could go deeper in places.

Research question
Clear and focused — could be sharper.
Structure
Well-organised. Ideas flow logically.
Knowledge
Good grasp of the topic and subject context.
Analysis
Mostly evidence-backed. Conclusions follow the research.
Sources
Relevant and appropriately used. Some critical evaluation.
Reflection (RPPF)
Clear, with signs of growth shown.

To reach A

  • Find where you describe instead of analyse — dig deeper.
  • Evaluate every source, don't just quote it.
  • Make your RPPF evaluative, not just a diary.
C

Grade C — Satisfactory

Research is present but the essay describes more than it analyses.

Research question
Present but may be too broad to answer fully.
Structure
Some structure, but inconsistent throughout.
Knowledge
Some relevant knowledge; wider context is thin.
Analysis
More descriptive than analytical. Conclusions partly supported.
Sources
Mostly appropriate. Terminology not always correct.
Reflection (RPPF)
Attempted. Some growth shown but not evidenced clearly.

Key actions

  • Narrow your RQ — apply the "so what?" test to every paragraph.
  • After each piece of evidence, explain what it proves.
  • Describe a specific turning point in your RPPF.
D

Grade D — Weak

Significant revision needed. The RQ is too broad and discussion is mostly descriptive.

Research question
Too vague — cannot be answered in 4,000 words.
Structure
Attempted but sometimes obstructs the argument.
Knowledge
Some relevant knowledge mixed with irrelevant content.
Analysis
Mostly descriptive. Conclusions inconsistent and unsupported.
Sources
Terminology used incorrectly. Sources applied weakly.
Reflection (RPPF)
Limited. Growth mentioned but not shown.

Priority actions

  • Rewrite the RQ — make it specific and answerable.
  • Ask yourself: am I describing or am I arguing?
  • Talk to your supervisor before revising further.
E

Grade E — Failing Fails diploma

The essay lacks focus, structure and analysis. Urgent action required.

Research question
Absent or completely unfocused.
Structure
No clear organisation of ideas.
Knowledge
Limited and partially accurate.
Analysis
Summarises sources only. No real argument.
Sources
Poorly used. Connections to argument missing.
Reflection (RPPF)
Very limited. No evidence of learning.

Urgent — see your supervisor now

  • Grade E means you cannot earn the IB Diploma.
  • Start with a new, specific RQ before anything else.
  • Read two strong EE examples in your subject.
